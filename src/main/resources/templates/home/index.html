    Endometriosis

    Endometriosis is a gynecological condition that often causes chronic pelvic pain or intense cramps during menstruation.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Endometriosis is a condition in which tissues similar to the lining of the uterus are also present elsewhere in the body, primarily in the abdominal cavity.

    You are more likely to develop endometriosis if you have:
    • Never had children.
    • Menstrual periods that last more than seven days.
    • Short menstrual cycles (27 days or less).
    • A family member (mother, aunt, sister) with endometriosis.
    • A health issue preventing normal menstrual flow.
    • Damage to pelvic cells caused by an infection.

    The most common symptom of endometriosis is pelvic pain. The pain often occurs during menstruation, ovulation, and/or sexual intercourse. However, a woman with endometriosis may also experience pain at other times in her menstrual cycle. Another symptom is infertility. Some women with endometriosis also report significant fatigue.

    There is no cure for endometriosis, but there are many treatments for the pain and infertility it causes. Talk to your doctor about the option that best suits you. The treatment you choose will depend on your symptoms, age, and pregnancy plans. Pain Medications - For some women with mild symptoms, doctors may suggest over-the-counter pain medications. Hormone Therapy - When pain medications are not sufficient, doctors often recommend hormonal medications to treat endometriosis. These medications can only be used by women who do not wish to become pregnant. Surgery - Surgery is typically the best option for women with severe endometriosis with many growths, significant pain, or fertility problems.

    Maintain lower estrogen levels in your body. Try:
    • Exercising regularly.
    • Maintaining a low body fat percentage.
    • Avoiding consuming large amounts of alcohol and caffeine-containing beverages.
